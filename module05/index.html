About the Game

Star Wars Jedi: Fallen Order is an action role-playing game developed by Respawn Entertainment and publsihed by Electronic Arts (EA) in 2019. Set 5 years after The Purge A.K.A Order 66, you follow the story of a young Jedi, Cal Kestis. The game is set in the early ages of the Empire, before Luke Skywalker even started training. The stroy is pretty direct, however if you explore enough you will find Force Echoes, which gives information of the past, not just the current story, rewarding those who choose to explore outside the beaten path.

The world in this game is connected in many ways. Often times you visit a planet multiple times, most you visit twice. Bogano and Ilum are the only outliers. You go back to Bogano 3 times, and you only visit Ilum once. Fallen Order is an excellent game for Star Wars fans. However, you dont have to be a Star Wars fan or know the lore to play the game and enjoy it. Everything in the game is it's own story, you don't need prior Star Wars knowledge to figure out what's going on. For more information about Fallen Order you can visit Wookiepedia or IMDb.

Why I Love Fallen Order

The primary reason I love this game is because of the fact that it is Star Wars. Star Wars is one of my favorite franchises ever, not much can top it. I also enjoy the fact that it is a higher difficulty, even playing on the easiest difficulty I was almost defeated multiple times. One of the most reqrding things is figuring out the puzzles and defeating enemeies. The reason defeating or enocuntering enemies is that you can scan the creatures with BD-1, your robot companion, after the enemies die. Why is scanning them important? Well when you open the menu it gives you a tactical guide that tells you the enemies' greatest weaknesses and strengths, but only when you scan them.

A major part of the design is how progression unlocks previously inaccessible locations. There are some parts you cannot get to right away, and have to return to that location later. For example, on the first visit to Kashyyyk, there is a certain secret that you cannot get to because you need a double jump and that ability is not granted until near the middle of the second Kashyyyk visit. Cal cut himself off from the force, but when you prgress in the story he has flashbacks to his training, each of which grant a new ability. Seeing these flashbacks is because of a unique power Cal has that not many other Jedi do called "Force Echos" which just means he can sense the past of objects he holds. The level design was inspired by Metroidvania because of all the locations you have to go back to after receiving abilities. This is jsut a small part of it, you can read more about the game at Wikipedia.

The game trusts in your ability to figure things out. It has quite a few puzzles you need to solve to progress, but it barely gives any information on them. You can request a hint from BD-1, but the hints barely give away much, you still have to figure out most of it. Sometimes I found the hints irritating because it only gave hints at things I already knew, and I still got stuck on some of the puzzles for a while. Especially the puzzle at the second Zeffo Sage's tomb, I couldn't figure it out without a guide. That puzzle is the hardest one to figure out, the first Zeffo Sage tomb is the second hardest.
